What we know about Betsy DeVos: A timeline

Trump's controversial pick for Secretary of Education was confirmed by the senate today. Here's a look back at her experience and qualifications to this point.

1958
Born
DeVos was born into one of Michigan's wealthiest families.
Early-mid 70s
Education
DeVos graduated from the private Holland Christian High School in Holland, MI and earned a BA in Political Science from Calvin College in Grand Rapids.
Early 1980s
Career origins
DeVos married the heir to Amway, Dick DeVos, and says that, during a visit as a new parent to the private Potter House Chrisitian School in Grand Rapids, was awakened to the power of school choice and charter schools
Read more...
Early 1990s
Charter schools
In 1993, DeVos and her husband fought for the successful passage of Michigan's first charter school bill and supported multiple organizations advocating for school choice.
2000
Voucher amendment defeated
The DeVoses pushed to amend Michigan's constitution, seeking to require the state to pay for tuition vouchers to students in districts where fewer than two-thirds of kids graduate from high school. Ultimately, the amendment was defeated despite $12.9 million provided in support by the DeVoses.
Read more...
2006
Gubernatorial effort
In 2006, Dick DeVos ran for governor of Michigan as a Republican. His platform included greater school choice options for the state.
Read more...
2011
Charter school expansion
In early 2011, Michigan lifted the cap on charter schools and created a pilot program for online charter schools. The associated bill was backed by the Great Lakes Education project, an inititative founded by the DeVoses.
Read more...
2016
Charter school backlash
In 2016, the New York Times reported on the negative impacts of uncontrolled charter school growth on Detroit public schools. This placed DeVos under scrutiny after her selection as Education Secretary by Trump.
